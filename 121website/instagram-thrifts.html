INSTAGRAM THRIFTS

Instagram thrifts or UKAY-UKAY

Since not all branded clothing can be afforded by the working class, ukay-ukay has been around for decades to give access to those who want to be stylish but can still afford clothes. This informal consumption of identity has built online thrifting instagram pages that sell prices over ₱150 used clothes (not including the shipping fee), rejecting the ukay-ukay culture. Because of the popularity of specific ukay-ukay styles like esoteric and goth, this has led for thrifting shops in instagram to buy, sell, and bid used clothes, having a negative impact on real-life ukay-ukay stores. I have been buying ukay-ukay clothes since my Junior year of high school and I would say that since the popularity of instagram pages selling thrifted clothes with specific styles has had high price ranges, the real-life ukay-ukay stores now also starts at ₱100, just lower than the instagram thrift stores. There are still ukay-ukay stores in real-life that hold that ₱20 mark, but these stores are usually located in provinces, making it almost the same prices as the shipping fee from the instagram thrift store and the transportation to the ukay-ukay store.

PERSON & CONSUMER THOUGHTS

AS A PERSON, since the romanticization of ukay-ukay has jumped into the popular TikTok and Instagram platforms, I found myself building a new wardrobe that has a specific style formed through used clothes and the "cubao expo outfit" inspired by instagram carousels. Since branded clothes don't have such specific styles, ukay-ukay has become one of the factors that shaped my persona as an artist brand that is unique and easy to remember.


AS A CONSUMER, the only branded clothing I can afford now with my allowance and freelance side gigs is Uniqlo, as they offer sales and affordable prices to students like me pricing around ₱300 to ₱600. When I got my allowance, the first thing I thought of was to spend a bit of it to ukay-ukay stores, due to the fact that not only is it affordable, but also I can buy several clothes with the same styles with low prices, unlike branded clothing where one croptop is equal to ₱300. Although ukay prices now start at ₱100, it is still worth it to have a lot of clothes that fit my taste. Since my ukay clothes have also become my brand as an artist, ukay-ukay has become my active laborer of fun. I check instagram thrift stores when I'm sad or frustrated, then go out to real-life ukay stores to find the same styles to find satisfaction and relief.

TOLENTINO'S CHARACTERISTICS DETECTED:

  • It is created for profit. Instagram resellers mostly created their accounts for high profit, taking advantage of the trending styles and adding a price amount only the middle class can buy.
  • Disseminated through Technology. This was started with popularization through social media platforms, specifically on Instagram and TikTok, so it continues to be promoted and trend overtime with technology.
  • Ascends to Sadomasochism. Since fashion trends change almost every season, the never-ending of consuming thrifts would result in sadomasochism.
  • Transgressive in Categories. The ukay-ukay or thrift ig reselling reaches social and cultural categories. For ukay-ukay, this allows a student or a working class to adopt high-fashion subcultures through "sachet economy" and with the middle-class fantasy mindset.
  • Comes Primarily from the Cities. The popular culture of ukay-ukay fashion trends mostly originated in Cubao or specifically, in Cubao Expo, an area in an urban city spreading its standards of "urbanity and cosmopolitanism" to the rest of the country.
