LATTES & MATCHA

Lattes & Matchas

The sudden romanticization of chilling into ambient coffee shops while you sip your matcha latte as you read the first chapter of your book has popularized through Instagram, combining aesthetic and Japanese tea cultures. Although this trend has blended in smoothly with normal lifestyle as generations go on, I consider the trend of posting lattes and matchas influential as they hold social statuses on how pricey coffees were and how peaceful place is that only middle class can afford (e.g. Starbucks), as where working class consider it as 'fantasies' or rather Tolentino's so called "Pakiwaring Gitnang Uri" where working classes often pretend or 'roleplay' to a lifestyle they can't afford. However, due to this trend arising, many coffee shops that provide ambience and affordable matchas have opened in the Philippines since then. Affordable prices ranging from ₱30 to ₱60 can now be experienced by the working class. Because of its influence, consumption of aesthetic of cafes and matchas have risen due to having access to this popular culture.

PERSON & CONSUMER THOUGHTS

AS A PERSON, I personally hate matcha with all my heart (haha, sorry) but I do like a peaceful cafe with pretty lights and a spanish latte to sip. I am thankful to the popularization of ambient cafes and matcha trends as I get to feel the sense of belongingness to something I can afford or do. Honestly, I go to a peaceful little coffee shop on my street to study and write on projects and because of this influence, I shaped my desk into a cozy space like the warmth the cafes I've been to gave me, where I was able to study more and focus on my work effectively unlike before.


AS A CONSUMER, I am aware the affordability of small cafe places are allowing me to partake in consumerist culture. I am still a student but most of my allowance goes towards getting a good spanish latte to study at a coffee shop. The aesthetics of combining a good coffee that wakes you up and studying at an ambient coffee shop portray a middle-class lifestyle I dreamed of yet to be.

TOLENTINO'S CHARACTERISTICS DETECTED:

  • The trend is created for profit. Many cafes had imitated the ambience of the popular trending cafes to gain money possessing high market value to a wide target market.
  • Transgressive in Category. Cafes and Matchas have a large target market and not only limits itself to one category, as it ranges with different drinks and dishes made for everyone.
  • Disseminated through Technology. This was started with popularization through social media platforms, so it continues to be promoted and trend overtime with technology.
  • Ascends to Sadomasochism. Since cafes vary from low price ranges to high price ranges, this creates a never-ending expectation and urge to consume a satisfaction of these expectations.
  • Comes Primarily from the Cities. One of the most popular cafes in the Philippines that holds a social status ranking is Starbucks, imported from America, Seattle, then started from the urban areas of our country.
