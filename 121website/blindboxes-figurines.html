BLINDBOXES & FIGURINES

Blindboxes & Figurines

It has come to my attention that Blindboxes like Sonny Angel, Skullpanda, and Labubu have gained popularity from the past year. Although it is already a popular culture in Japan where it is a New Year tradition called Japanese fukubukuro ("lucky bag"), the mystery bags and gachapons have attracted lots of attention for having rare collectibles and hidden collections. Popular blindboxes like Labubu from POPMART have a starting price of ₱900 to ₱1,200 back in 2024, targeting the middle-class population. As the blindboxes continue to evolve, the hidden figurines are duplicated or mass produced to lower quality for the working class to afford, dropping from ₱900 to ₱300 or ₱600. Some local stores like MINISO also added to their stores blindbags with a starting price of ₱100, while National Book Store and MR. DIY blindboxes starting at ₱50. Although the original Smiski glow in the dark official figurine found in Fully Booked stores costs at ₱500- ₱600, you can buy a fake smiski on TikTok shop for only ₱100.

PERSON & CONSUMER THOUGHTS

AS A PERSON, I've been a fan of blindboxes for a while now. Not just because of the beautiful designs, but also because of feeling a sense of thrill when you start to open the box or bag, like a gambling experience. I am one of the few people who enjoys the dopamine-driven thrill of uncertainty, whether it ends bad or good, I live for the thrill and feeling of it. The influence of blindboxes built me to be dependent on figures being my comfort when I am numb or disappointed with something else, as it also gives me the emotions of 'good anxiety' leading to addiction.


AS A CONSUMER, it's toxic. Blindboxes have become a symbol of status. When you have a rare Labubu hanging on your sling bag or a collectible MLP Skull Panda in your purse, you're known to be in either high or middle-class. Popular blindbox pendants are known to be expensive so hanging one just by your purse in the open means it might get stolen, unless you're in a private vehicle or in a prestigious school that everyone can afford an expensive pendant. Although I don't have any expensive blindbox collections, I bought the cheaper ones to 'fit in' the anik-anik aesthetics. Just like my ukay-ukay consumer experience and coffee shop addiction, I consume blindboxes for the sake of my sense of belongingness to the middle-class and active labor of fun.

TOLENTINO'S CHARACTERISTICS DETECTED:

  • It is created for profit. Blindboxes are mass-produced, expecting that consumers will buy because of its design and appealing characteristics. The creation of blindboxes are created for profit due to the high prices tagged to it, targeting the middle class to maximize profit. With the mass production of lower-quality duplicates priced lower than the original prices shows targeting a wide audience instead of just focusing on the middle class.
  • Disseminated through Technology. Just like the romanticisation of cafes and ukay-ukay fashion, the popularization of blindboxes was started through TikTok and Instagram reels. Getting the attention of an audience with rare secret designs or a newly released official figurine from POPMART.
  • Ascends to Sadomasochism. Due to the gambling experience and the thrill or anxiety the consumer feels when opening a blindbox, the consumer's satisfaction is never fully satisfied, creating addiction.
  • Transgressive in Categories. From the high priced Labubu to the Mr. DIY cheap blindbags, this allows the wealthy and the working class to engage with the same cultural icons, albeit at different price points.
  • Comes Primarily from the Cities. This popular culture disseminated from the prestigious urban areas or global hubs.
